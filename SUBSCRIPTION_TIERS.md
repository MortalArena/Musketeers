## Pricing Strategy

### Value Proposition

**Free Tier**:
- Low barrier to entry
- Enables experimentation
- Builds user base
- Converts to paid tiers

**Basic Tier**:
- Affordable for small teams
- Sufficient for production use
- Provides essential features
- Good value for price

**Pro Tier**:
- Professional features
- Scalable for growth
- Advanced capabilities
- Competitive pricing

**Enterprise Tier**:
- Custom solutions
- Premium support
- Compliance ready
- Dedicated resources

### Pricing Psychology

**Anchoring**:
- Pro tier at $99/month makes Basic tier at $29/month seem reasonable
- Enterprise tier is custom, allowing for higher pricing

**Decoy Effect**:
- Pro tier offers significantly more value than Basic
- Encourages upgrade to Pro instead of Basic

**Loss Aversion**:
- Free tier users fear losing features when upgrading
- Emphasize what they gain, not what they lose

### Revenue Model

**Projected Revenue** (per 1,000 users):
- Free: 500 users × $0 = $0
- Basic: 300 users × $29 = $8,700
- Pro: 150 users × $99 = $14,850
- Enterprise: 50 users × $500 (avg) = $25,000
- **Total**: $48,550/month

**Conversion Rates**:
- Free to Basic: 30%
- Basic to Pro: 25%
- Pro to Enterprise: 10%

## Upgrade/Downgrade Policies

### Upgrades
- Immediate effect
- Prorated billing for remaining month
- New limits applied immediately
- No additional fees

### Downgrades
- Effective at end of billing cycle
- No prorated refunds
- Old limits applied until cycle end
- Data retention for 30 days after downgrade

### Cancellation
- Effective at end of billing cycle
- No prorated refunds
- Data retention for 30 days after cancellation
- Reactivation within 30 days restores data
